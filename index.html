Welcome to the Redesign project for Allara Health!

Our team collaborated to redesign the dashboard for Allara Health: a virtual care platform specializing in women's hormonal and metabolic health, particularly, conditions like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

We received a few specs based on the difficulties patients experience.

The Current Dashboard
Section Details
Static Care Timeline Informational block for expectation setting (disappears after four appointments, collapsable)
To-Dos Collect information from patients: Share home address, lab preferences, join appointments, download mobile app, review nutrition materials
Appointments Modify appointments, add details to calendar, access provider notes post-appointment
My Health View past appointments, book follow-up visits, see lab orders/results, download PDFs, track progress (e.g., walking 10,000 steps, drinking two liters of water)
Booking Appointments Medical follow-up, nutrition follow-up, confirm state, see availability, book with dietician
Messaging Message concierge team and providers, start new threads, select topics, concierge team routes messages to providers or answers them

The major pain points that we had to account in our redesign were:

  • - Chaotic nature and oversaturation of information on the dashboard
  • - Users being unsure on where to proceed and how to join appointments
  • - Patients having difficulty accessing post-visit notes

Sketches

To start the redesign, we first need to focus on brainstorming some sketches based on the Allar's needs from above. Our team did not look at the existing design prior to brainstorming but focused our efforts to solve the pain points.

Wireframes
The next step is to create a set of wireframes based on the sketches above.
 
Key notes:

- Dashboard separated into three main blocks - appointments, to-dos, and lab results.

- Timeline moved to the bottom with expandable buttons for new patients.

- Filter for upcoming and past appointments, join button closer to appointment time, directions for in-person meetings.

- More information for past appointments, including notes and recommendations.

- Easy access to different sections.

- Added progress bar and reward system to enhance user engagement and retention.

First round feedback
Liv Jenks, the Product Manager from Allara, and Caroline Walters, a Portfolio Operations Manager at GV, commented on our wireframe design and provided essential feedback!

Positive changes

Modular design is great and makes the layout more approachaba

Moving the timeline is a good idea, but requires additional work

The addition of progression is amazing

Things to note

Need to have a more forward-looking approach with a dynamic timeline

No appointments are ever in person

Lab results are rather infrequent, so should not be prioritized on the dashboard

Final Prototype

After receiving a lot of positive feedback as well as pointers to work on, we felt excited to implement our ideas in Figma. Together, we developed an interactive Figma prototype to convey our vision for Allara’s patients.

Main Features

Dashboard

  • - To-Dos component is the highest priority element
  • - Appointments and health goals are in the middle of the screen
  • - Every component is interactable and leads to its own dedicated page

Appointments

  • - Join button increases in size and changes color for upcoming appointments
  • - Clear distinction between past/upcoming appointments
  • - Simple navigation to get to notes or lab results

To-dos

  • - The goals and to-dos have progress bars to gamify the process
  • - To-Dos separated into popup screens
Final Feedback from Liz Jenks

Positives

  • - Clean up font sizes and different type styles.
  • - The sections are very clear.

Suggestions for UI

  • - Clean up font sizes and different type styles.
  • - Make the dashboard more scannable.

Suggestions for CTAs (Call to Actions)

  • - Currently, there are four different primary CTAs, which is overwhelming
  • - Consider creating more space between elements.
  • - Use different CTA states (secondary, tertiary) to make it cleaner.
Conclusion

Approaching the Allara health dashboard redesign iteratively allowed our team to stay closely aligned with user needs while adapting to the complex nuances of women's chronic health management. Through a cycle of research, prototyping, testing, and refinement, we were able to uncover key insights about user behavior, expectations, and pain points—many of which may have been overlooked in a more linear process.

This method fostered meaningful collaboration with Allara product personnel and Google Ventures, resulting in a dashboard experience that feels more intuitive, supportive, and empowering. Ultimately, the iterative approach enabled us to balance usability with empathy, creating a design that truly reflects Allara’s mission of accessible, clear, patient-centered care.
