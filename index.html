Welcome to the Redesign project for Allara Health!

Our team will collaborate to redesign the dashboard for Allara Health a virtual care platform specializing in women's hormonal and metabolic health particularly conditions like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

We received a few specs based on the difficulties patients experience.

The Asks
Section Details
Static Care Timeline Informational block for expectation setting (disappears after four appointments, collapsable)
To-Dos Collect information from patients: Share home address, lab preferences, join appointments, download mobile app, review nutrition materials
Appointments Modify appointments, add details to calendar, access provider notes post-appointment
My Health View past appointments, book follow-up visits, see lab orders/results, download PDFs, track progress (e.g., walking 10,000 steps, drinking two liters of water)
Booking Appointments Medical follow-up, nutrition follow-up, confirm state, see availability, book with dietician
Messaging Message concierge team and providers, start new threads, select topics, concierge team routes messages to providers or answers them

The major pain points that we had to account in our redesign were:

  • Chaotic nature and oversaturation of information on the dashboard
  • Users being unsure on where to proceed and how to join appointments
  • Patients having difficulty accessing post-visit notes

    • Observations

    To understand the user experience of the website, I took notes on its memorability, efficiency, learnability, conceptual model, and accessibility

    Efficiency
    - The layout is overly spread out, requiring excessive scrolling to access content on the left and right sides of the page.
    - Despite this, the interface is simple and easy to navigate.
    Memorability
    - The minimalistic design lacks contrast, color differentiation, and size variations, making it visually unmemorable.
    - There is no clear visual hierarchy, making it harder to quickly identify important elements.
    - Buttons and icons do not have distinct styling to indicate they are clickable, which may cause confusion.
    Learnability
    - The interface provides clear instructions, helping users understand how to interact with key elements such as the calendar.
    - However, a brief description of the pageâ€™s purpose could improve clarity and help users understand their tasks more easily.
    Conceptual Model
    - The calendar feature effectively visualizes selected dates, making it intuitive for scheduling.
    - However, beyond the calendar, the site lacks a clear conceptual model to guide users in understanding how different elements function together.
    Accessibility Concerns
    - The absence of headings and defined page regions may make it difficult for screen readers to navigate the site efficiently.
    - Many elements are missing labels, which makes it harder for assistive technologies to interpret content.
    - The calendar is not keyboard-accessible; users can only select dates via mouse clicks, creating usability barriers for those who rely on keyboard navigation.
    - These limitations could significantly impact users with accessibility needs, leading to frustration and difficulty navigating the site.
